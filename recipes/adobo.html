FILIPINO STYLE ADOBO

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made with meat, usually chicken or pork, marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. The meat is then simmered until tender and served with rice.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken or pork, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Add the meat and marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the marinated meat (reserve the marinade) and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Pour the reserved marinade into the pot with the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the meat is tender.
  4. Adjust seasoning with salt if needed. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Notes

You can add potatoes or hard-boiled eggs to the adobo for variation. Some people also like to add coconut milk for a creamier sauce.

Enjoy your Filipino Style Adobo!
